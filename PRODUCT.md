# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Misty Atelier is for visitors browsing a fictional illustrated archive: art-book readers, visual collectors, and people looking for quiet seasonal image worlds. They arrive to browse atmosphere, follow curated routes, inspect works, and move between exhibitions without feeling like they are using a generic gallery grid.

## Product Purpose

Misty Atelier presents local illustrated artworks as an online art book. Success means the first screen feels authored, the exhibition pages feel curated, the archive remains searchable, and individual work pages preserve image focus while providing enough context to continue browsing.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, editorial, atmospheric.

The interface should feel like a restrained visual archive: paper-like, tactile, careful, and image-led. It should preserve a soft atelier mood without becoming washed out, sentimental, or template-like.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like a SaaS dashboard, a stock image grid, a default Next.js starter, a dark neon anime portal, or a card-heavy marketing template. Avoid oversized decorative gradients, generic glassmorphism, heavy shadows, purple-blue AI defaults, and repeated tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section unless the label genuinely aids navigation.

## Design Principles

1. Let the artwork lead. Text, motion, and controls should support image inspection rather than compete with it.
2. Curate before indexing. Bulk imports should be accessible, but the homepage and route page should remain deliberately selected.
3. Use restraint with enough contrast. The atmosphere can be soft, but body text and navigation must remain readable.
4. Make routes feel authored. Navigation should imply a walk through rooms, weather, studio hours, and fables.
5. Keep the system expandable. New image groups should enter through generated data and holding spaces before they become featured curation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive elements. Preserve keyboard-accessible links and controls, clear focus states, reduced-motion behavior, readable line lengths, and responsive layouts down to mobile widths around 390px.
